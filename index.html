Methodology

Design thinking is a non-linear, iterative process that teams use to understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions to prototype and test. Involving five phases—Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype and Test—it is most useful to tackle problems that are ill-defined or unknown.

Design thinking value as a world-improving, driving force in business (global heavyweights such as Google, Apple and Airbnb have wielded it to notable effect) matches its status as a popular subject at leading international universities. With design thinking, teams have the freedom to generate ground-breaking solutions. Using it, your team can get behind hard-to-access insights and apply a collection of hands-on methods to help find innovative answers.

Empathize

In the first phase, it is about investigating and understanding the problem for which we want to find a solution. The keys are to listen and empathize. When you focus on your audience and care about meeting their needs, you are able to come up with innovative solutions that really impact their lives.

Think

It is time to focus on the problem. By analyzing the information and observations collected in the first phase, you will be able to state the problem and begin to devise how to deal with it by defining an objective.

Mockup

In this phase it is about creating multiple ideas, as many as you can. So you will have a battery of options to choose from. Make use of all your creativity and your capacity for innovation, because you will need it. Here the key is to look for new alternatives, new solutions to the usual problems.

Test

Prototyping is giving shape to ideas. Prototypes are an intermediate step, prior to the final solution. Experiment with ideas, invent, build, communicate, identify options.

Implement

It's time to see how your solutions work. The staging is key in this phase. Generate immersive experiences in the context in which your solutions will be used or, at least, in an environment as similar as possible to help understand the solution you propose.
